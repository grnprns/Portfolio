Icon Design

Sustainability App

Icon designs for sustainability app: Design for two different user groups.

Brainstorming & Sketching

For starters, I thought of metaphors and ideas for each option. I was trying to come up with less traditional, common metaphors. Then I started sketching out these ideas and looking for references, e.g., for animals.

Styles & Moodboard

Before getting into the design proper, I had to think of target groups and fitting styles. The first target group is more affluent car owners. You could imagine these icons being part of e.g. a BMW app. The style is elegant, modern and monochrome. The first target group is more affluent car owners. Final Designs

I created some guidelines in Adobe Illustrator to base all my designs off for consistency’s sake. It also just helps with the placement of elements. With the help of these guides, I created both icon sets. One uses bolder lines, while the other is more delicate.

Final Screens

Lastly, I brought it all together with fitting background images, colours and fonts. The first background image is by Annie Splatt, the second one is by Johann Siemens, both found on unsplash.com. Fonts used are Domus Light and Exo Soft Regular.

Final Thoughts

Coming up with exciting, yet still recognisable, metaphors for the concepts was tough, but I had a lot of fun sketching them out. Creating different styles for each set was also not an easy feat, but I’ve learned how helpful moodboards are.
